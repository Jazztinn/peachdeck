# Design QA

Use this reference before delivering a visual deck file or detailed slide design spec.

## Visual Direction

- Design around one clear idea per slide.
- Use the product, customer workflow, chart, screenshot, or diagram as the primary visual whenever possible.
- Make the headline the takeaway and the visual the proof.
- Keep layouts restrained: strong grid, consistent margins, limited type styles, and clear hierarchy.
- Avoid decorative visuals that do not clarify the business.

## Slide Density

Recommended limits for a 16:9 presentation:

- Headline: 4-10 words.
- Main body: 35 words or fewer when possible.
- Bullets: 2-4, each one line when possible.
- Chart: one message, direct labels, minimal legend.
- Screenshot: crop to the relevant workflow and annotate only the key action.

If a slide needs more detail, split it or move the detail to appendix.

## Typography And Layout

- Use large, readable type. Do not rely on speaker zoom.
- Keep title placement consistent across slides.
- Use whitespace to group related ideas.
- Align content to a grid; avoid arbitrary floating elements.
- Keep footer metadata small and quiet.
- Ensure contrast is strong enough for projection.

## Color And Imagery

- Use a small, purposeful color system: background, text, accent, success/warning if needed.
- Do not let one accent color dominate every object.
- Use real product screenshots, customer artifacts, market maps, or generated visuals that depict the actual idea.
- Avoid stock-like imagery that could fit any startup.
- If using icons, keep them from one visual family and pair them with short labels only when needed.

## Data Visualization

- Title the chart with the conclusion.
- Show units, time periods, and source or basis.
- Remove gridlines and labels that do not help the point.
- Use direct labels instead of legends when possible.
- Do not use 3D charts, decorative gauges, or unscaled pictograms.

## Speaker Notes

Speaker notes should add spoken context, not repeat every word on the slide.

- Include the first sentence the presenter should say.
- Include the evidence or story behind the headline.
- Include expected objection handling for fundraising decks.
- Keep notes concise enough for rehearsal.

## Final QA Checklist

Before delivery:

- Confirm the first two slides explain what the company does and why it matters.
- Confirm every slide has one job.
- Confirm every claim has evidence, a stated assumption, or a clear follow-up.
- Confirm team, traction, market size, and ask are specific enough for the audience.
- Confirm all text fits without overlap or crowding.
- Confirm charts and screenshots are legible at presentation size.
- Confirm visual style is consistent across the deck.
- Confirm appendix material is not blocking the main story.
- If a rendered preview is available, inspect it before final response and fix visible issues.
